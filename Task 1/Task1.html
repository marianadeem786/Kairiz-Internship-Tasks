Tribute to Imran Khan

Imran Khan, born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan, is a former international cricketer and the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan. Renowned for his illustrious cricket career, Khan led Pakistan to its first Cricket World Cup victory in 1992. Following his retirement from cricket, he entered politics and founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party. Known for his philanthropic efforts, he established the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre and Namal University.

Achievements

Imran Khan has accomplished numerous feats throughout his career:

  • Captained Pakistan to its first Cricket World Cup victory in 1992.
  • Founded the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre, Pakistan's largest tertiary care hospital.
  • Established Namal University to provide higher education to underprivileged students.
  • Elected as the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan in 2018.
